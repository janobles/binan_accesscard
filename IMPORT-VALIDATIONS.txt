===============================================================================
 FAMILY EXCEL IMPORT - VALIDATION REFERENCE
 Binan Access Card (CSWD)
===============================================================================

HOW THE IMPORT WORKS
--------------------
1. You upload the filled .xlsx.
2. The system READS and CHECKS it. Nothing is saved yet.
3. The Import Review screen lists every problem it found.
4. You fix the problems IN THE SPREADSHEET and upload again.
   (The review screen is read-only on purpose. The spreadsheet is the single
   source of truth - fixing it in the browser would leave the mistakes in the
   file, and the next person to use that file would import them all over again.)
5. When there are no ISSUES left, press Confirm import.


WHAT THE REVIEW SCREEN SHOWS
----------------------------
Six tiles across the top:

    Family groups       how many families the file contains
    Total members       every person in the file
    Ready to import     families that are CORRECT and will be saved
    Already in system   families that already exist and will be SKIPPED
    Issues to fix       blocking problems - the import will not run
    Warnings            things to read, but which do not stop the import

Then the problems, grouped by type (or by row - there is a toggle), each naming
the exact Excel cell. Click a cell to copy it, then press Ctrl+G in Excel and
paste to jump straight to it.

Finally the READY TO IMPORT list: every family that is correct, with its head,
member count and address. This is the other half of the picture - the rest of
the screen is nothing but problems, so this is where you confirm the good data
really is good before you commit.

A family only appears there if the import would genuinely CREATE it. Families
that are blocked, already on file (skipped), or being added to an existing
family are deliberately left out - listing them would be a promise the import
does not keep. A family with warnings only DOES appear, marked "imports as
typed", so nothing is glossed over.


THE TWO SEVERITIES
------------------
ISSUE TO FIX (blocking)
    Stops the import. "Confirm import" stays disabled until it is gone.
    Either the data is wrong, or the database would physically refuse it.

WARNING (informational)
    Does NOT stop the import. It tells you what the import is about to do, or
    points at something that looks like a typo. Read them - some of them mean
    rows will be SKIPPED and not saved.


===============================================================================
 ISSUES TO FIX  (blocking - the import will not run)
===============================================================================

FILE
    The file could not be read at all.
    Catches: not an .xlsx, wrong sheet, missing column headers, corrupt file.

EMPTY
    The file was read but has no family rows under the header.

QR-11 - Merged QR cells
    The QR Number column has merged cells in the data area.
    Catches: a worker merging one QR across a family's rows instead of
    repeating it. Merged cells hide the QR from every row but the first.
    (The template's decorative banner at the top is ignored - only merges that
    reach the data rows are flagged.)

QR-01 - Missing QR Number
    The QR Number cell is blank.
    Catches: a forgotten QR. Every person needs their family's QR on their row.

QR-FORMAT - Invalid QR Number
    The QR is not a plain whole number.
    Catches: letters ("ABC123"), decimals ("5880.0"), commas ("6,001"),
    negatives ("-1"), scientific notation, stray symbols.

QR-05 - QR Number is zero
    The QR is 0. Zero is not a valid card number.

QR-07 - QR Number too large
    The QR is above 2,147,483,647 (the database column's ceiling).
    Catches: a slipped keyboard producing a 12-digit number.

QR-08 - QR Number is an error cell
    The cell holds an Excel error value like #REF! or #N/A.
    Catches: a broken formula or a bad paste.

QR-12 - QR Number is a formula
    The cell holds a formula (=A4) instead of a typed number.
    Catches: a dragged/filled formula. The value can change out from under you.

QR-TAKEN - QR belongs to someone else        [NEW]
    That QR number is ALREADY used by a DIFFERENT family in the system.
    Catches: THE MISTYPED QR. One wrong digit (6001 instead of 6091) files a
    brand-new family onto a stranger's card number.
    Two forms:
      - the QR is on file under a completely different person; or
      - the same name, but the birthday does not match what is stored.
    Blocking because it CANNOT be saved: a QR belongs to exactly one family
    (it is the primary key), so the import can neither skip it (this head is
    new) nor insert it (the number is taken). Left as a warning it would fail
    halfway through the import instead of being caught here.
    Fix: correct the QR, or give the new family its own unused QR.

HEAD-NONE - No Head in the family
    A QR group has no row with Relationship = Head.
    Catches: a forgotten Head, or a family whose Head landed on another QR.
    The message tells you who is most likely the Head, using the address (only
    the Head fills in Address/Barangay, so the person with an address is
    almost always the Head).

HEAD-MULTI - More than one Head
    A QR group has two or more Head rows.
    Catches: a copied block, or two households sharing one QR by mistake.

FP-ADDR - Two addresses under one QR
    Rows sharing one QR carry different addresses or barangays.
    Catches: a copy-pasted block, or a row shift that dropped a stranger's
    household onto this QR. One QR = one household.
    Fix: correct the mistyped QR, or give the other household its own QR.

REQUIRED - Missing required value
    A required cell is blank.
    Head needs: Civil status, Education, Job, Address, Barangay.
    Everyone needs: QR, Relationship, First name, Last name.

BDAY - Invalid birthday
    The birthday is not a real date, or is missing on a Head.
    Format: MM-DD-YYYY.

SEX - Invalid sex
    Not Male or Female.

INCOME - Invalid monthly income
    Not a bracket label from the dropdown, and not a number.

SERVICE - Unknown service code
    A service code that is not on the Reference sheet.

LENGTH - Value too long
    The value would not fit the database column and would be cut off silently.
    Limits: First/Last name 100, Middle name 50, Civil status 100,
            Contact number 20, Religion 100.


===============================================================================
 WARNINGS  (the import still runs - but READ THESE)
===============================================================================

DUP-EXISTS - Already in the system
    Same QR AND same Head (name + birthday) as a family already on file.
    This is a genuine re-upload. The family is SKIPPED - it is not imported
    twice, and nothing is duplicated.
    Note: this now checks the PERSON, not just the QR. It only says "already in
    the system" when the head really is the same human being.

DUP-DIFF - Details differ from the system      [NEW]
    Same family as above, but your file disagrees with what is stored (a new
    phone number, a corrected middle name, etc).
    Catches: someone editing the spreadsheet expecting the import to UPDATE the
    record. It does not. Families already on file are SKIPPED, so those edits
    would be silently thrown away.
    Fix: edit the record in Manage Family instead. The message lists exactly
    which fields differ, and both values.

DUP-DB - Person already in the system          [NEW]
    This person is already on file, under a DIFFERENT QR.
    Catches: THE SILENT SKIP. A head re-entered under a brand-new QR used to
    look perfectly clean in the review - then the import quietly dropped the
    whole family (head, members, everything) because the head already existed,
    and nobody was told.
    If the person is a HEAD: the whole group will NOT be saved. Check the QR.
    If the person is a member: they will be added as a second record. If it is
    really the same person, delete the row.

ADD-MEMBER - Will be added to an existing family
    A group with NO head whose QR already belongs to a family = members being
    added to that family.
    Catches: the forgotten-member case. A worker finishes a family, then
    remembers a member the next day and adds the row in a later batch.
    These people are ADDED automatically on import. To skip one, delete their
    row from the file.
    (If the person is already in that family, they are NOT added twice - you
    get a DUP-DB warning instead.)

DUP-PERSON - Possible duplicate person
    Two rows in YOUR FILE look like the same person: same first + middle + last
    + suffix + birthday, AND the same household address.
    Catches: the same person typed twice. Two real people can share a name -
    but never a name, a birthday, AND an address.
    Imports anyway, on purpose. Delete a row if it really is a duplicate.

BRGY - Barangay not recognised
    Not one of the official Binan barangays.
    Catches: a spelling slip. The Barangay dropdown has no "Other" option, so
    anything off-list is suspect. Matching is tolerant (Sto./Santo, n/n-tilde,
    "(alias)" ignored). Imports as typed.

CONTACT - Contact number format
    Does not start with 09, or is not 11 digits.
    Imports as typed.

SUFFIX - Suffix adjusted
    The suffix was mapped to the matching dropdown value ("Junior" -> "Jr",
    "the 3rd" -> "III"). If it matches nothing, it is left blank.
    The database only accepts Jr, Sr, I, II, III, IV, V.

BDAY-RANGE - Birthday out of range
    A valid date, but implausible: in the future, or over 150 years ago.
    Catches: a mistyped year. 150 is well past the oldest human on record
    (~122), so it cannot flag a real person. Imports anyway.

QR-CONTIG - Family rows not together
    One family's rows are not next to each other in the sheet.
    Catches: a sort or a paste that scattered a family. The family still
    imports correctly - this just asks you to check the grouping.


===============================================================================
 QUICK ANSWER: "same QR" - when is it a problem?
===============================================================================

NOT a problem - this is the design:
    A whole family shares ONE QR. The head and every member carry the same QR
    number on their row. That is how the importer groups them into a family.

        QR     Relationship   Name
        6001   Head           Juan Cruz
        6001   Spouse         Maria Cruz
        6001   Child          Jose Cruz          <- correct

A problem - the QR collides with the DATABASE:

    Your file's QR is already in the system as...   Result
    ------------------------------------------------------------------
    the SAME head, same details                     DUP-EXISTS (warning)
                                                    re-upload, skipped
    the SAME head, but your file's details differ   DUP-EXISTS + DUP-DIFF
                                                    (warning) skipped, your
                                                    edits are NOT saved
    a DIFFERENT person                              QR-TAKEN (BLOCKING)
                                                    mistyped QR
    the same name, DIFFERENT birthday               QR-TAKEN (BLOCKING)
                                                    would fail on import

Only the cases that genuinely cannot be written are blocked. Re-uploads stay
warnings and import fine.
